I am a MSc student at the WCPM, where I work on interdisciplinary fields of maths and science on the MSc in Predictive Modelling and Scientific Computing. My MSc thesis supervisor is Prof. James Kermode.

Alongside this, I work on an open-source implementation of neural state estimation methods at Kalmanix, and I have broader interests in deep learning, reinforcement learning, agent-based modelling, and mechanistic interpretability.

Research

My current research explores differentiable simulation pipelines for inverse problems, drawing on ideas from statistical mechanics, uncertainty quantification, and numerical optimisation. More broadly, I am interested in scientific machine learning approaches that remain mechanistically grounded, uncertainty-aware, and computationally efficient.
